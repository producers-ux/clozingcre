The Building · Its History

Arthur Elrod
1924 – 1974

Arthur Elrod arrived in Palm Springs in 1947 and spent the next two decades defining what the desert interior could be. His firm, Arthur Elrod & Associates — established on Palm Canyon Drive in 1954 — shaped the interiors of the most celebrated mid-century homes in the Coachella Valley, working alongside architects including John Lautner, William Cody, and Donald Wexler.

His client list read like the guest list of the era: Lucille Ball and Desi Arnaz, Walt and Lillian Disney, Hoagy Carmichael, Bob Hope. He was described as the “design king of the desert” — the person who understood, better than almost anyone, how light and space behaved in the Sonoran heat.

850 N Palm Canyon Drive was home to his studio. Open + Range is activating the building in 2026 — the first public programming of the space in decades. Still Light at 850, the lie-down meditation and sound series, runs through the summer and fall. Launch date confirmed upon building owner approval.
